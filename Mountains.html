Amboli

Amboli

Also known as the 'Queen of Maharashtra', Amboli is one of the lesser-visited hill stations of Maharashtra. It is situated in the Sahyadri hills in the Sindhudurg district of Maharashtra and receives heavy rainfall in the monsoon, making the climate pleasant and covering the ground with lush green foliage.

Toranmal

Nandurbar

Perched at a height of 3770 ft. above sea level, Toranmal hill station is located in the Satpura range of Maharashtra. Blessed with scenic charm, Toranmal is about 305 kilometres away from Nashik and 200 kilometres away from Surat. The lush green surroundings and tranquil lakes make it perfect for a relaxing weekend getaway, especially during the monsoons.

Igatpuri

Nashik

Located in the Nashik district of Maharashtra, Igatpuri is one of the most popular hill retreats in the state. About 130 kilometres away from Mumbai, this weekend getaway is ideal for adventure sports junkies and nature lovers.

Bhimashankar

Bhimashankar

Bhimashankar is a popular hill station owing to the religious significance of one of the 12 Jyotirlingas being present in the region. Located about 100 km away from Pune and 223 km away from Mumbai, Bhimashankar is surrounded by magnificent hills and lush green forests.

Lonavla-Khandal

Khandala

Located in the Pune district of Maharashtra, these are twin hill stations that have been a popular spot for tourists for decades. Also known as the 'Jewel of Sahyadri', Lonavala city is only about 3 km away from Khandala.

Malshej Ghat

Malshej

With its numerous lakes, waterfalls, and mystical valleys, Malshej Ghat is an ideal spot from the clamour of city life. A popular weekend retreat from Mumbai and Pune

Mahabaleshwar

Mahabaleshwar

Nestled in the picturesque Western Ghats, Mahabaleshwar is one of the most popular hill stations in Maharashtra. The scenic views from this beautiful hill station can captivate your senses and will instantly make you fall in love with its magnificent cascades and majestic peaks.

Panchagani

Mumbai

Named after the five surrounding hills, Panchgani is a renowned hill station in the state of Maharashtra. Developed by the British empire, Panchgani was more like a retirement place as it was pleasant and calm all through the year..

Wai

Satara District

Daulatabad alias Devagiri fort is near the city of Aurangabad. The city as well as the fort was well fortified. The citadel of the fort is located on the top of a hill.

Lavasa

Pune

Lavasa city is India's first planned hill city. This new hill station is a beautiful project that takes inspiration from the Italian town, Portofino. Spread over 7 hills, covering about 25000 acres, Lavasa offers a luxurious escape from city life.

Bhandardara

Bhandardara

Situated beautifully amidst the Sahyadri ranges in Maharashtra, Bhandardara is a beautiful hill station for nature lovers. Often called the Queen of the Sahyadri ranges for its lush green landscapes

Chikaldara

Chikaldara

The sole hill retreat in the Vidarbha region, Chilkaldara is perched at a height of 1,118 metres above sea level. Located in the Amravati district of Maharashtra, Chikaldara boasts of a mention in the epic text of Mahabharata.

Gagan Bavda

Kolhapur

An offbeat weekend getaway, Gagan Bavda is located only 55 kilometres away from Kolhapur. It is located on the Western Ghats and is known to receive heavy rainfall in the monsoons, covering the entire region with lush green foliage and a climate that city dwellers yearn for.

Mhaismal

Chincholi

Located near Maheshmal village, the lesser-known Mhaismal hill station in Aurangabad district offers you the perfect break away from the crowds. At a remarkable height of 1067 metres above sea level, this little weekend getaway is about 40 km away from Aurangabad city. Also known as the 'Mahabaleshwar of Marathwada', Mhaismal is a perfect blend of breathtaking terrains and awe-inspiring nature.
